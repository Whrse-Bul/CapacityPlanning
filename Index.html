Process Review & Milestones

The plan follows the physical warehouse flow. Capacity must be balanced at every handoff to prevent queues, congestion, rework, and missed dispatch.

End-to-End Warehouse Flow

Each stage has a different control point and failure risk.

1

Inbound Receiving

Unload, count, document, and stage. Control arrival sequence and receiving completion.

→
2

Quality Control

Inspect and release good stock. Isolate variance, damage, or failed items quickly.

→
3

Put-away / Storage

Protect location availability, avoid staging congestion, and maintain stock accessibility.

→
4

Fulfillment

Pick, consolidate, and complete requests using released and location-ready stock.

→
5

Outbound

Verify, pack, document, stage, and dispatch before the cutoff.

Inbound requirement

Plan by arrival volume, unloading window, staging space, and receiving HC. Excess receiving without QC capacity creates a queue.

QC requirement

QC has the lowest hourly standard, so it commonly becomes the release bottleneck. Prepare inspection space and escalation rules.

Fulfillment requirement

Use demand volume, backlog, pick path, and stock readiness. Protect enough HC to prevent store or customer requests from rolling over.

Outbound requirement

Work backward from dispatch cutoff. Packing and document completion should be ahead of vehicle loading time.
